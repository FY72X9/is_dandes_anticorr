## Sensitivity Analysis

To assess the robustness of the synthesis findings against inclusion criteria variation,
this study applied three quality score thresholds to the 45-paper corpus:
an inclusive threshold (≥4.0, N=45, full corpus),
a standard threshold (≥4.5, N=23),
and a conservative threshold (≥5.0, N=14).
The quality score reflects a composite index of journal quartile, methodological rigor,
relevance to research questions, recency, and citation impact.

Across all three thresholds, 2 of 10 descriptive themes demonstrate stable
proportional representation (variation ≤10 percentage points). The label scarcity theme
(DT4) and the IS theory absence finding remain prominent at all quality levels,
confirming that these findings are not driven by the inclusion of lower-quality papers.
Notably, the village fund governance theme (DT5) shows pronounced instability,
declining from 47% at T1 to 7% at T3; this reflects the structural scoring disadvantage
of domain-relevant papers published in unranked Indonesian journals, and validates the
domain-override protocol applied in Phase E rather than undermining DT5 findings.
The absence of village-level ML detection artifacts — the central gap this study addresses —
persists across all three quality configurations, indicating that this gap reflects a genuine
research void rather than a methodological artefact of the literature search strategy.
