Title:
Corruption Indication Detection in Village Fund Expenditure Activities Using Comparative Unsupervised Machine Learning: Evidence from Jambi Province, Indonesia

Scope of Research :
This research operates at the intersection of Information Systems and public financial management, focusing on Jambi Province, Indonesia. The study applies three distinct unsupervised machine learning paradigms — Isolation Forest (IF), Local Outlier Factor (LOF), and Robust Deep Autoencoder (RDA) — to 99,692 activity-level village fund expenditure absorption records extracted from the SIMDA Desa government financial information system spanning fiscal years 2023–2025. The analytical scope encompasses seven engineered financial features derived from documented corruption modus operandi (mark-up, fictitious projects, completion manipulation, and procurement irregularities), multi-paradigm consensus anomaly identification, corruption typology mapping, and village-level priority tier classification. Theoretical interpretation is grounded in the Fraud Triangle, Principal-Agent Theory, and the DeLone and McLean IS Success Model. Outputs are designed to produce actionable ranked inspection guidance for Aparat Pengawas Internal Pemerintah (APIP) at the kabupaten and provincial level.

Abstract:
The Program Dana Desa channels about Rp 71 trillion annually to 75,259 villages across the country; however, Indonesian Corruption Watch reports 591 judicial decisions related to a national loss amounting to Rp 598.13 billion from corruption on village fund allocations, implying that there is a fundamental flaw within the monitoring mechanism that remains reactive and happens several years after such financial abuse. This research develops and evaluates the unsupervised learning pipeline for anomaly detection within expenditure data of fund absorption activities before further development into the next funding cycle. Three different algorithms—Isolation Forest (IF), Local Outlier Factor (LOF), and Robust Deep Autoencoder (RDA)—are applied on transaction activity records at the system level SIMDA Desa of the Province of Jambi for fiscal year 2023 to 2025. This pipeline is expected to operationalize the chain of information quality impact to organizational impact determined by the IS success model of DeLone and McLean, offering a triage tool for effective and systematic monitoring of village funds by the Government Internal Control Agency (APIP).

The Dana Desa scheme distributes Rp 71 trillion yearly amongst 75,259 villages in Indonesia, however, Indonesian Corruption Watch lists 591 judicial rulings that have accounted for Rp 598.13 billion in confirmed government losses arising from corruption cases involving village funds. This study designs a novel and evaluates a comparative unsupervised machine learning model capable of detecting anomalous expenditures in village fund absorptions prior to their accumulation over subsequent payment cycles. The three dissimilar algorithms used are: Isolation Forest (IF), Local Outlier Factor (LOF), and Robust Deep Autoencoder (RDA). Each model is trained on activity-level transaction records amounting to 99,692 samples derived from the provincial government of Jambi, from SIMDA Desa system for the fiscal periods 2023-2025. These anomalous indicators are quantified into seven engineered features which capture seven types of corruption activities as described in the literature: mark-up detection, fictitious projects, manipulation of completion, and procurement irregularities. Using a multi-paradigm anomaly detection technique, 3,107 highly likely anomalous transactions (3.1%) have been identified for prioritization into 642 target villages. Of these models, LOF generates the highest bimodality ratio at 0.957 which implies greater ability to differentiate between normally distributed expenditure categories and local outlier records – enabling the capture of category-based price inflation which cannot be achieved with global categorisation techniques. The corrupt practices mapping reveals 50.6% T1 and 50.5% T7 patterns. The designed ML algorithm is a direct application of the information quality-to-organisational impact chain outlined in the DeLone and McLean IS success framework. APIP inspectorate can now utilise such tool for data-driven triaging of village fund management activities, ensuring effective compliance with mandated audits.
