Village fund corruption in Indonesia represents a persistent governance failure: between 2015 and
2023, the Corruption Eradication Commission (KPK) documented over 600 cases involving Dana Desa
disbursements totalling more than IDR 433 billion. Despite accelerating advances in machine
learning (ML)-based financial fraud detection, no study has applied these methods to the specific
institutional and data conditions of Indonesian village fund governance. This systematic literature
review (SLR) maps and critically synthesises the existing evidence base to identify what ML
methods exist, what feature engineering frameworks have been validated, and what structural gaps
preclude direct application to Dana Desa anomaly detection.

Following PRISMA 2020 guidelines, this review screened 1,001 records retrieved from OpenAlex,
Scopus, IEEE Xplore, and Web of Science, ultimately including 45 papers published between 2018
and 2026. Thematic synthesis, bibliometric cluster analysis, and a Design Science Research (DSR)
framework matrix reveal four analytical themes: (1) an Operationalization Chasm — no study bridges
ML detection methods with village fund governance; (2) a Scalability Illusion — reported high
performance figures do not generalise to low-volume, unlabelled sub-national data; (3) an Absence
of IS Theory — 62% of technical papers contain no information systems theoretical grounding; and
(4) a Ground Truth Paradox — circular validation on synthetic data cannot support deployment claims.

The gap matrix identifies three critical research voids: no ML artifact exists for Dana Desa data;
no validated feature set addresses village fund corruption typology; and label scarcity precludes
supervised learning approaches. These findings directly motivate and frame a primary empirical
study applying an unsupervised ensemble (Isolation Forest, LOF, Autoencoder) to real Jambi
Province disbursement data (2023–2025).

**Keywords**: village fund corruption; machine learning; anomaly detection; systematic literature
review; information systems governance
