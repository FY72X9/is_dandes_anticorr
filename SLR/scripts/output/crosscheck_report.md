## 7. Pipeline Scoring Bias — Critical Finding

**Finding**: The pipeline quality scoring systematically undervalues domain-specific
papers from developing-country IS journals because:

| Dimension | Impact on Dana Desa Papers |
|---|---|
| `score_journal_quality` | Small Indonesian journals (unranked in SCImago/JCR) default to 2.0/10 |
| `score_citation_impact` | Recent domain papers (2023–2026) have citations < 20, scoring 2.0–5.0 |
| `score_relevance_to_rq` | Pipeline uses fixed 4-point scale; domain papers score 3.0 not 10.0 |
| `score_methodological_rigor` | Non-ML governance studies (quantitative survey) score 3.0 not 7.0 |

**Net effect**: High-domain-relevance papers score 4.15 (below 5.5 include threshold)
while ML cybersecurity papers in IEEE Access score 6.6–8.25.

**Implication for SLR methodology section**: Document this bias as a LIMITATION and
justify the domain-relevance override protocol for RQ2/RQ3 papers explicitly.
Cite precedent: Petticrew & Roberts (2006) advocate purposive sampling alongside
systematic search when domain-specific evidence bases are thin.
